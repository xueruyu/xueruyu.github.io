I taught a breadth of CS topics (e.g, block-based programming language and Java) and math topics (e.g, algebra, function, and trignometry) in public high schools since 2018. I strive to create learner-centered experiences that encourages exploration and expression.

Example CS project: Sci-Fi Name

This project uses Trinket and is part of an introductory project-based CS curriculum for grade 9 students. It asks students to generate a personalized sci-fi name for users and engages students with string, variable, concatenation and agile software development process. I adapted the project by simplifying e-textbook instructions, adding visuals for scaffolding and transforming into Google Slide workbooks. Below is a sample student work.

Example AI project: Daisy Model

This project is part of a two-week Data Science curriculum developed by MIT for grade 9 students. Students reflected on critical aspects that defined them and shared with each other. They learned how to categorize, clean, format, and visualize data by combining identity information into a group dataset in Google Sheets. I adapted the project by providing concrete examples for some steps.
